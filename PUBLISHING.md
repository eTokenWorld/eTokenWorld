# Publishing Rules

**Public-Safe Publishing Rules**  
**Project:** eTokenWorld

---

## Purpose

These publishing rules define what may be shared publicly and what must remain protected.

The purpose is to support public understanding of eTokenWorld while avoiding disclosure of protected technical details, confidential commercial information, sensitive operational matters, strategic development direction, or non-public implementation work.

---

## Public-Safe Content

The following categories are generally suitable for public release:

- high-level project vision
- public-facing positioning
- public-safe roadmap summaries
- general ecosystem direction
- partnership-facing communication
- opportunity and growth messaging
- non-confidential public documentation

---

## Protected Content

The following categories must not be published publicly unless specifically approved:

- confidential implementation details
- internal system design
- non-public methods or workflows
- internal scripts, tools, or utilities
- sensitive security or control procedures
- protected commercial logic or strategy
- non-public deployment methods
- internal engineering materials
- confidential operational materials
- invention-sensitive materials
- trade secret information
- non-public roadmap, rollout, or planning details

---

## Writing Rules

Public-facing materials should:

- remain high level
- stay clear and commercially professional
- avoid unnecessary technical detail
- avoid explaining protected differentiators
- avoid exposing internal business logic
- avoid disclosing strategy that reduces competitive advantage
- avoid language that invites technical reverse interpretation

Preferred qualities:

- clear
- restrained
- credible
- public-safe
- business-relevant

---

## Claims Rules

Do not publish claims that:

- overstate technical readiness
- imply approvals, adoption, or deployment that do not exist
- reveal protected novelty before appropriate protection is in place
- expose strategic intentions unnecessarily
- disclose non-public implementation or commercial mechanics

All public claims should be intentional, supportable, and limited to approved public-safe scope.

---

## Naming Rules

Use approved public-facing names only.

Examples of acceptable public naming may include:

- eTokenWorld
- QoreMesh
- trusted participation
- ecosystem coordination
- human-centered opportunity
- future-facing digital development

Do not publish internal-only naming, restricted technical labels, or protected terminology that has not been approved for public use.

---

## Security Rules

Never publish:

- secrets, keys, credentials, or access data
- internal defensive procedures
- non-public enforcement or control logic
- sensitive diagrams or internal system maps
- incident handling details
- private runtime materials
- unpublished operational methods

---

## Repository Rules

Public repositories should not contain:

- internal runtime scripts
- protected logic
- sensitive configuration
- confidential test artifacts
- private operational playbooks
- internal deployment bundles
- non-public engineering materials

Only intentionally approved public-safe materials should be placed in public repositories.

---

## Review Questions

Before publishing, ask:

1. Is this public-safe?
2. Does this reveal protected technical or commercial information?
3. Does this reduce strategic advantage?
4. Does this expose non-public implementation direction?
5. Would this help a competitor understand too much too quickly?

If yes to any of the last four questions, do not publish without approval.

---

## Default Rule

When in doubt, do not publish.

---

## Scope Reminder

This repository is intended for public-safe communication only.

Protected implementation, internal methods, confidential strategy, non-public development work, and invention-sensitive materials must remain outside the public repository.
