# Publishing Rules

**Public-Safe Publishing Rules**  
**Project:** eTokenWorld  
**Powered by:** QoreMesh OS  

---

## Purpose

These publishing rules define the boundary between materials that may be shared publicly and materials that must remain protected, private, or restricted.

The goal is to support public understanding without disclosing confidential implementation details, protected inventions, internal security practices, or sensitive commercial information.

---

## Public-Safe Content May Include

The following categories are generally appropriate for public-facing publication:

- high-level vision and positioning
- public-safe project descriptions
- public-safe roadmap summaries
- collaboration invitations
- non-sensitive partnership framing
- non-confidential ecosystem direction
- general documentation for public understanding
- high-level user or builder orientation
- non-sensitive branding and communication materials

---

## Protected Content Must Not Be Published

The following categories must not be published in public repositories, websites, decks, social posts, demos, or public documentation unless explicitly approved:

- confidential implementation details
- internal architecture not designated public-safe
- proprietary execution methods
- unpublished technical mechanisms
- internal scripts and operational tooling
- internal security procedures
- protected economic logic
- protected monetization mechanics
- private infrastructure controls
- non-public deployment methods
- internal data structures
- invention-specific internals
- patent-sensitive disclosures
- trade-secret materials
- internal strategy, rollout, or defense plans

---

## Writing Rules

Public-facing writing should:

- stay high level
- remain clear and professional
- avoid over-explaining technical mechanisms
- avoid disclosing non-public business logic
- avoid revealing hidden differentiators
- avoid providing operational details that reduce strategic advantage
- avoid statements that imply features are live unless confirmed for public release

Preferred style:

- vision-led
- public-safe
- non-confidential
- partnership-friendly
- precise without being revealing

---

## Naming Rules

Use approved public-facing names only.

Examples of acceptable public naming:

- eTokenWorld
- QoreMesh OS
- New World Gateway
- User Gateway Access
- trusted digital participation
- governed services
- ecosystem coordination

Do not introduce internal-only naming, experimental naming, restricted architecture labels, or protected terminology not approved for public release.

---

## Claims Rules

Do not publish claims that:

- disclose technical novelty before protection is in place
- overstate readiness, scale, or deployment status
- imply regulatory, institutional, or commercial approval that has not been obtained
- reveal strategic plans not intended for public knowledge
- expose internal utility, control, or economic mechanisms

All public claims should be supportable, intentional, and appropriately limited.

---

## Security Rules

Never publish:

- secrets, keys, credentials, or internal endpoints
- operational security procedures
- detection methods
- internal enforcement logic
- sensitive trust or control mechanisms
- private incident procedures
- unpublished architecture diagrams that expose defensive posture

---

## Code and Repository Rules

Do not place in public repositories:

- internal runtime scripts
- sensitive configuration
- non-public automation
- protected protocol logic
- internal test artifacts that reveal design internals
- confidential deployment materials
- proprietary operational playbooks

Public repositories should contain only materials intentionally cleared for public-safe release.

---

## Review Rule

Before publishing, ask:

1. Is this public-safe?
2. Does this reveal protected architecture or business logic?
3. Does this reduce strategic advantage?
4. Does this disclose something that should remain patent-protected or trade-secret?
5. Would this help a competitor understand too much too quickly?

If the answer to any of the last four questions is yes, do not publish without explicit approval.

---

## Approval Principle

When in doubt, do not publish.

Sensitive or borderline content should be reviewed internally before any public release.

---

## Scope Reminder

This repository is for public-safe communication only.  
Protected implementation, internal methods, confidential strategy, and invention-sensitive materials must remain outside the public repository.
