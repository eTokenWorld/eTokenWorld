# Publishing Rules

**Public-Safe Publishing Rules**  
**Project:** eTokenWorld  
**Built on:** QoreMesh infrastructure

---

## Purpose

These rules define what may be published publicly and what must remain protected.

The goal is to support public understanding of eTokenWorld without disclosing sensitive technical, commercial, strategic, or invention-related information.

---

## Public-Safe Content

The following categories are generally suitable for public release:

- high-level project vision
- public-facing positioning
- non-sensitive roadmap summaries
- general ecosystem direction
- public-safe partnership framing
- high-level builder and participation messaging
- non-confidential communication materials

---

## Protected Content

The following must not be published publicly unless specifically approved:

- confidential implementation details
- internal architecture
- proprietary execution methods
- internal scripts or tooling
- unpublished technical mechanisms
- private security procedures
- protected commercial strategy
- monetization logic
- utility mechanics
- internal deployment methods
- internal data structures
- invention-sensitive materials
- trade-secret information
- non-public rollout plans

---

## Writing Rules

Public-facing materials should:

- remain high level
- stay commercially professional
- avoid unnecessary technical detail
- avoid explaining protected differentiators
- avoid exposing internal business logic
- avoid disclosing strategy that reduces competitive advantage

Preferred qualities:

- clear
- restrained
- credible
- public-safe
- commercially relevant

---

## Claims Rules

Do not publish claims that:

- overstate technical readiness
- imply approvals that do not exist
- reveal protected novelty before appropriate protection is in place
- expose strategic intentions unnecessarily
- disclose non-public infrastructure or commercial mechanics

All public claims should be intentional, supportable, and limited to public-safe scope.

---

## Security Rules

Never publish:

- secrets, keys, credentials, or endpoints
- internal defensive procedures
- non-public enforcement logic
- unpublished trust or control methods
- sensitive system diagrams
- incident handling details
- private runtime materials

---

## Repository Rules

Public repositories should not contain:

- internal runtime scripts
- protected protocol logic
- sensitive configuration
- confidential test artifacts
- private operational playbooks
- internal deployment bundles
- non-public engineering materials

Only intentionally cleared public-safe materials should appear in public repositories.

---

## Review Questions

Before publishing, ask:

1. Is this public-safe?
2. Does this reveal protected technical or commercial information?
3. Does this reduce strategic advantage?
4. Does this expose invention-sensitive material?
5. Would this help a competitor understand too much too quickly?

If yes to any of the last four, do not publish without approval.

---

## Default Rule

When in doubt, do not publish.

---

## Scope Reminder

This repository is intended for public-safe communication only.

Protected implementation, internal methods, non-public architecture, confidential strategy, and invention-sensitive materials must remain outside the public repository.
